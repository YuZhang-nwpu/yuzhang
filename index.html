About me

I am now teaching graduate courses in "Advanced Operating Systems," Wireless Communication and Mobile Computing," "Mobile Health Perception and Computing in Special Environments," and undergraduate courses in "Computer Operating Systems (Bilingual)," "Operating System Design," "Internet of Things Technology Frontier I", "Internet of Things Technology Frontier II," and “Intelligent Computing System.” I am also doing research in Adaptive management of heterogeneous sensing and communication resources for unmanned swarm, wearable edge intelligence for health perception and transmission in special environments.
Networked Embedded Heterogeneous Sensing Resource Runtime Management: This project focuses on the development of abstract methods for heterogeneous ubiquitous sensing resource integration and the construction of a capability-based adaptive management model for human-machine-thing fusion collective intelligence systems. It involves cognitive assessment and performance evaluation of heterogeneous sensing resource capabilities at runtime, dynamic real-time intelligent selection of distributed sensing resources, unified access adaptation and software-hardware co-design for heterogeneous sensing resources, as well as research on formal verification methods for robustness in embedded neural network models. Smart perception and transmission of mobile wearable health in special environment: It is mainly aimed at the special environment of aviation, aerospace, long-distance navigation and low oxygen at high altitude, carrying out the continuous monitoring of human physiology and the major strategic needs of the country for early intervention, carrying out the low-power data transmission strategy of large and small scale, the dynamic assessment, prediction and guarantee of multi-modal wearable health status in special environment, and the natural human-computer interaction research of the ubiquitous operating system across scenarios and domains.
